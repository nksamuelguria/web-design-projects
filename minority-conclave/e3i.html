Why E3i Model?

In 2016 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) succussed the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs)

Though the goals have been set for all the countries and a number of efforts have been made to get it implemented, The 2022 SDG implementation report shows that the implementation of SDGs are getting delayed may be due to Covid or because the developing and under-developed countries are facing challenges in the implementation.
(Annexure 1: https://unstats.un.org/sdgs/report/2022)

Developing countries are finding it difficult to implement the SDGs and specifically the one related to Climate and Environment. In fact, the climate tracker shows that most of the countries of the world are delayed in the implementation of Climate-related SDGs.
(Annexure 2: https://climateactiontracker.org/countries/usa)

To achieve the implementation of SDGs in time-bound manner and to achieve the same result globally, there is a need to implement a model which should be followed by all the countries whether it is the Developed, Developing or Under-Developed Countries.

The efforts have been made during COP26 or through ESG model to get it implemented but the results show that there is no equal progress among the developed, developing and under-developed countries.
(Annexure 3: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0959652618320754?via%3Dihub)

(Annexure 4: https://www.livemint.com/news/india/climatechange-a-big-challenge-for-most-developingcountries-pm-modi-at-cop26-summit-11635781221750.html)

(Annexure 5: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/developingcountries-sustainable-development-goals-sdgsambrose)

The Goals can only be implemented and result can be achieved when all the nations enshrine the goals into their policy and practises and E3I model discusses about same.

Educate

There is a need to include climate change adaptation policies in school syllabus to make next generation aware of issues, COP26 Summit, October 2021 (Shri Narendra Modi, Honourable Prime Minister of India)

The courses and curriculum should be aligned with the sustainable development goals which are related to the Global Warming, Climate Change, Water and Forest Conservation and Partnership.

The courses which are concentrating on solving such problems should be given importance in the future global educational strategies and agendas.

While around 452 million children were enrolled in secondary school in 2000, the number reached about 601 million pupils in 2019.
(Annexure 6: https://www.statista.com/statistics/1227098/number-of-pupils-in-secondary-educationworldwide)

Today, there are around 220 million tertiary education students in the world, up from 100 million in 2000.
(Annexure 7: https://www.worldbank.org/en/topic/tertiaryeducation)

The same should also be included in the School education so that the upcoming generation should be ready to adopt and implement the SDGs into their lifestyle and also contribute towards society at global level.

Link for the SDG courses all around the globe:-

Introduction of the SDG courses and curriculum at school level will enhance the implementation and mindset globally as the one- tenth of population are in the secondary and tertiary education.

Innovate

Though the efforts have been made in terms of sustainable development and the product innovations towards achieving the Green Climate and environment, the result shows that the diversity is too much and the global competitiveness is too diverse on this. We have analysed the Innovation on the below given figure and found too much diversity among countries and lack of cohesiveness in implementation.
(Annexure 9: https://solability.com/the-global-sustainable-competitiveness-index/the-index)

Thus, cohesiveness and the accepted level of sustainable innovation can only be achieved through the combined efforts of Institutions and Industry.

Implement

Industry is the third pillar which is a vital part to make this model successful. The innovation done by the universities should be in collaboration with the Industry so that the Innovations can be accepted by the industry and they should scale it.

Success of any model depends on the harmonical acceptance of it by all the stakeholders of the society.

The data shows that the level of implementation is too diverse for the countries as they are working in nuclear way.

(Annexure 10: https://climateactiontracker.org/countries)
(Annexure 11: https://epi.yale.edu/epi-results/2022/component/epi)

Thus, the implementation strategy is required where all the countries should agree on a same model and implement the same. For the implementation of the same, E3I model is the solution and a forum will be created which will discuss and finalize the roadmap for the implementation of this model universally.

Impact

While implementing all the three previous stages, it should be kept in mind that it should impact the society in a positive and measurable manner.

There is a need to evaluate the impact of the last three stages on a regular basis and then need to revaluate and reassess the model on regular intervals.
