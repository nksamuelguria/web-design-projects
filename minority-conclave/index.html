Under the visionary and exemplary leadership of Hon’ble Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi, India now stands tall in the comity of nations as the largest democracy with multi-religious and multi-ethnic communities. Shri Narendra Modi has always given prime importance to the welfare and upliftment of six minority communities in India to build ‘Ek Bharat, Shreshtha Bharat'.

Espousing Shri Narendra Modi’s vision of an inclusive and egalitarian society, NID Foundation is organizing a first-of-its-kind event titled "All India Minority Conclave - Role of Minorities in Amrit Kaal" in the union territory of Chandigarh on February 20, which is observed as the World Day of Social Justice.

The conclave will bring together prominent leaders from all religious groups, renowned academicians and actors, industry leaders, eminent sportspersons, and distinguished scholars to facilitate extensive and fruitful deliberations and gain insight into the significant role of minorities in Amrit Kaal. The event, divided into two sessions, will provide an ideal platform to pave the way for ensuring sustainable, equitable, and inclusive growth in the country.

Themes

01

Minorities in India:
Representation, Recognition and Participation

Read More

It is crucial to democracy’s functioning that the constitutionally mandated legal framework be put into practice to uphold minorities’ representation and participation in the development process of the country. Thus, there is a need to analyse a range of critical issues pertaining to minorities’ representation and participation in the decision-making process in Amrit Kaal.

02

Minorities in India:
Inclusion and Empowerment

Read More

The approach of the 3Es—education, employment, and empowerment—adopted by the Government of India to ensure the inclusive growth of minorities has marked a significant departure in the relationship between the regime and the country’s minorities. It is vital that the authorities take a proactive lead to identify and bridge the prevailing gaps in the protection of rights to further empower the minorities.

03

Minority Perspective: Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas

Read More

With the motto "Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas”, Shri Narendra Modi has shaped a new era of politics while focusing on social cohesion and nation-building. Ensuring the socio-economic-educational empowerment of religious minorities while recapturing the constitutional vision of an inclusive and egalitarian society is essential for building a new India.
